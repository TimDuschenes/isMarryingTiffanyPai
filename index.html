Travel

Carlton Towers, Carlton, North Yorkshire, DN14 9LZ. Alternative postcode for satnav: DN14 9LW

Flying In

1. Flying into Manchester

— There are direct flights from New York (JFK) to Manchester through British Airways, Aer Lingus and Delta.

— We suggest taking the Transpennine Express from Manchester Picadilly. There is a direct train to Selby that will take one and a half hours for around £23.

2. Flying into London

— There are multiple direct flight options from New York (JFK) to Heathrow through British Airways, American Airlines, Delta, Virgin Atlantic, Jet Blue.

— If you are to fly into London, there is a direct train from Kings Cross to Selby via Hull Trains for around £100.

Driving In

From North Yorkshire

A1 followed by M62 (Junction 33, Knottingley). Exit M62 at Junction 34 (Whitley Bridge/ Selby), second left onto the A19.

Proceed for approximately a mile and take a right turn (sign posted Snaith/Goole) and continue along this road for approximately 5 miles. Entering Snaith, turn left at the mini roundabout (A1041 Selby Road).

Continue on this road for 1.25 miles with the entrance to Carlton Towers being on the right-hand side, just after the lake.

From West Yorkshire

M62, take Junction 34 (Whitley Bridge/ Selby), second left onto the A19. Proceed for approximately a mile and take a right turn (sign posted Snaith/Goole) and continue along this road for approximately 5 miles.

Entering Snaith, turn left at the mini roundabout (A1041 Selby Road). Continue on this road for 1.25 miles with the entrance to Carlton Towers being on the right-hand side, just after the lake.

From the South

M1 – M18, take Junction 6 (A614 Thorne/ Selby) continuing on the A614 for approximately 6 miles. Turn left towards Snaith (sign for Carlton Towers) and proceed for 2.5 miles before turning right (A1041 Selby Road). Continue on this road for 1.25 miles with the entrance to Carlton Towers being on the right-hand side, just after the lake.

From East Yorkshire

M62, take Junction 36 (Goole) and turn right (A614 Snaith), continue for approximately 1 mile before turning onto A645. Continue until the first roundabout (Drax Power Station) before turning left.

Continue on this road until it becomes the A1041 (approximately 1 mile).

Turn left (Carlton/Snaith) and within 1 mile enter the village of Carlton. The entrance to Carlton Towers can be located as you exit the village, on the left-hand side.

Sights

Attractions nearby

York

York is thirty minutes away by car from Carlton. It was founded in AD71 by the Romans and was captured in 866 by the Vikings.

Famous Attractions

  • York Minster
  • York City Walls
  • Brimham Rocks
  • The Shambles
  • York Viking Museum

Yorkshire Dales

The Yorkshire Dales and North York Moors are National Parks. They are famous for limestone formations and miles of underground caves. Wensleydale cheese is from the Dales region.

The Lake District

The Lake District is England’s largest national park and a UNESCO world heritage site. It features 12 of the largest lakes in England, and has inspired writers the likes of Beatrix Potter and John Ruskin.

Type set in Catalogue by Lineto. It is a contemporary update of a 19th century serif font of Scottish origin.

The typeface was originated by Alexander Phemister in 1858 for London / Edinburgh based office Miller and Richard, which ultimately closed in 1952 due to the advent of hot metal typesetting.

The extremely straight and robust typeface allowed for an accelerated printing process, more economical production, and more efficient mass distribution in the age of Manchester capitalism.

Alex Phemister would later move to the United States and later popularize the use of old style serifs stateside.
